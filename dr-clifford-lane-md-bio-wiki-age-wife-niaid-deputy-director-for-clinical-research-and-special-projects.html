Lan started his career at NIAID in the year 1979. He at that time worked as a clinical associate in the Laboratory of Immunoregulation (LIR). Lane was appointed MIAID Deputy Clinical Director in the year 1985. Lane became the chief of the LIR Clinical and Molecular Retrovirology Section in 1989. This is the position he still holds up to date. Lane became clinical director of NIAID in 1991`. Lane assumed his current positions as the NIAID Deputy Director for Clinical Research as well as Special Projects in the year 2006. Lane as well functions the NIAIS liaison with the U. S. Clifford’s early work in the laboratory involved studies aimed at dissecting the normal immunoregulatory mechanisms that control the human immune response to specific antigen challenges. Clifford became one of the first investigators to study the immunopathogenic mechanism of HIV Disease when the AIDS Epidemic emerged. He made seminal observations that really helped establish the field of HIV immunopathogenesis.

Clifford has used investigational therapeutic interventions to further the understanding of HIV pathogenesis. Lane pioneered the strategies of immunologically compatible bone marrow transplantation as well as the adoptive transfer of lymphocytes. Lane has as well examined the roles of cytokines in treating patients with HIV infection.
