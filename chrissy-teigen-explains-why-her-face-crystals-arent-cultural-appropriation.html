Chrissy Teigen Explains Why Her 'Face Crystals' Aren't Cultural Appropriation

Publish date: 2024-08-27

What would you do with an IDGAF attitude and the face of a supermodel? Chrissy Teigen's got both, and is choosing to stick crystals all over herself.

Unlike Spencer Pratt and other new age celebs, Chrissy doesn't seem to be filling her life with crystals for any divine spiritual reasons. She's doing her own thing! The creative, sparkly looks she's serving on her socials are attracting a lot of attention - both positive and negative.

She Faced Backlash for a Bindi-Style Look

On pictures posted from her first Shabbat (a feast honoring the Jewish day of rest), Chrissy got criticism for wearing items that have deep cultural significance to some of her followers.

Fans noticed she had a single diamond-shaped crystal between her eyebrows, which looked like the type of 'bindi' worn by people who follow Hindu and Jain religions. To them, the bindi often represents a 'third eye' that wards off bad luck. It can also be used to show a woman's marital status.

Chrissy also wore a white shawl that seemed to be in the style of a Jewish woman's head covering. Orthodox women in the Jewish tradition often wear a 'mitpachat' of this variety to show their modesty, and may sometimes use 'tallit' prayer shawls depending on their community's rules.

As Chrissy was neither Hindu, Jain, or Jewish as far as the public knew, people expressed concern that she was using these items for fashion purposes.

Chrissy Says It's Not Appropriation

When the comments about her appearance started to overtake her socials, Chrissy spoke up. She Tweeted that her crystal was not a bindi, because it was part of a look that included crystals around her eyes which were less visible. She also said her head covering would have been worn by her Jewish grandmother, revealing that she does in fact have Jewish roots.

She Admits the Crystals Are Sometimes a Mistake

"If u would like to follow my crystals on the face journey, please like and subscribe to my Instagram or cry about it," Chrissy's Twitter thread continued. She also shared a clip of her cuddling her son, and wrote "I will however admit these crystals were a mistake (but miles is so cute and not a mistake)."

It's true that Chrissy's IG feed is absolutely full of crystal-covered selfies. She's taken to putting them on other people, too.

Harmless or shady? One thing everyone can agree on: this mama looks like she's having a good time.
